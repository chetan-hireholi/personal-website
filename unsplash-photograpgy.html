Featured on Unsplash Editorial!

I've always liked the idea of open licensing photographs to the public. I remember when in high school, during my powerpoint presentation assignments, I hated using images with watermarks. I always hunted for wesbites which gave me super HD - quality images for wallpapers, projects, etc. Then I stumbled upon Unsplash. It instantly became my one-stop gallery wallpapers and inspiration to develop my own style.

Anyone can upload their images to Unsplash. So did I. On my trip to Nepal, on an adventure like Bilbo Baggins, I started clicking photos (too many photos, yes lots of photos, some 2500+ photos). It turns out, not always which looks perfect on the camera's TFT screen turns out to be exactly the same when you enlarge on your laptop. Sometimes you miss the focus and sometimes you f'up the brightness. (OH YES, YOU CAN CLICK PICS BEING ON AUTO_MODE), but hey, where's the fun in that!

After returning home, I started shortlisting pictures which I can upload to my Instagram handle (shoutout, my handle: tranquilhuman) Found out, there were just couple of photos which were perfectly lit and framed. But I was not sad. It was a great learning experience. Ofcourse, I did my YouTube tutorials before and during my journey, but on-the-field is where you do the real learning.

As a Computer Science Engineer, I started to plan my journey in photography like I plan an algorithm. I then realised my roadmap towards clicking good pictures:

  • Step 1: Get used to your camera. Build up the muscle memory of the buttons and features. This will only happen if you click loads of images.
  • Step 2: Once you got trained on your camera, start clicking pictures (manual mode) which are balanced. Rightly lit, focussed where you wanted and framed appropriately
  • Step 3: Get to know your genre. It can nature, street, B&W, motor, etc. and start building towards telling some sort of a message from your clicks. The viewer must understand what are you trying to tell via your images... He/She must understand what went through your mind while you clicked an image. Its like art.
  • Step 4: Repeat step 3
